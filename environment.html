Draft Environmental Policy for Cross River State

Introduction

This Draft Environmental Policy is aimed at enhancing sustainable development and improving the quality of life in Cross River State through responsible environmental management.

Objectives

  • Promote sustainable development while protecting the environment.
  • Reduce environmental risks and ensure cleaner, healthier surroundings.
  • Guide all levels of government, businesses, and citizens in eco-conscious decisions.

Scope of the Policy

The policy applies to both public and private sectors, covering:

  • Biodiversity conservation
  • Climate change response
  • Land use and natural resource planning
  • Forestry and water protection
  • Pollution control and waste management

Principles Guiding the Policy

  • Polluter Pays Principle
  • Precautionary Principle
  • Equity and social inclusion
  • Community Participation
  • Responsibility and Accountability

Policy Instruments

  • Environmental Impact Assessments (EIA)
  • Strategic Environmental Assessments (SEA)
  • Environmental Audits and Monitoring
  • Payments for Ecosystem Services (PES)
  • Research, Education, and Public Awareness

Implementation Framework

The policy implementation involves coordination between the Cross River State government, local governments, NGOs, the private sector, and communities. The Ministry of Environment will oversee enforcement, compliance, and reporting.
